# Task 2: Customer Segmentation Analysis

Level: 1

## 📌 Project Overview
This project applies K-Means clustering to an e-commerce dataset to group customers based on their purchasing behavior, income, and engagement channels. The goal is to provide actionable insights for targeted marketing and strategic business decisions.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack
* **Python:** Data manipulation and analysis.
* **Pandas & Scikit-Learn:** Data scaling and K-Means machine learning algorithms.
* **Matplotlib & Seaborn:** Visualizing data distributions and customer clusters.

## 💡 Key Insights & Customer Segments
Using the Elbow Method, we determined the optimal number of clusters is **4**. 

1. **Segment 0 (The Average Consumer):** Moderate income (~$53k) and average spending habits.
2. **Segment 1 (The Budget Shoppers):** Lowest average income (~$32k) with minimal total spend (~$62). 
3. **Segment 2 (The Tech-Savvy Spenders):** Solid income (~$65k) with high spending (~$954). This group records the highest number of web purchases.
4. **Segment 3 (The VIP In-Store Buyers):** The highest earners (~$77k) and highest overall spenders (~$1,377). They heavily prefer in-store shopping over digital channels.

## 🎯 Business Recommendations
* **Digital Campaigns:** Target Segment 2 (Tech-Savvy Spenders) with exclusive online discounts and email marketing, as they are the most active on the web.
* **Premium In-Store Experience:** Focus luxury product placement and personalized in-store services towards Segment 3, as they drive the highest revenue and prefer physical shopping.
* **Entry-Level Promotions:** Engage Segment 1 (Budget Shoppers) with bundle deals or basic necessities to gradually increase their lifetime value.
